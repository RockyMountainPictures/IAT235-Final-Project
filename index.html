about us

about us

The Carnaval de Québec is a vibrant and enchanting winter festival held in Québec City, Canada. We bring a diverse range of activities, parades, and cultural events that celebrate the spirit of Québécois culture and the beauty of the winter season.
When?

Where?

our vision

The 67th Carnival is a continuation of the new vision, but adapted to the exceptional situation due to the pandemic. It will focus its efforts on the traditional and essential elements that have always been part of its history: to break the gloom of winter and stimulate the local economy, while bringing festivities into play.

meet bonhomme

Bonhomme is the iconic snowman mascot representative of Carnaval de Québec who has become a symbol of the carnival, embodying the spirit of the festive event. Born in 1954, Bonhomme holds tradition and heritage for he represents Québec’s winter culture and identity through his presence and physical appearance. With Bonhomme at the carnival, he helps to unite the community and attract visitors from around the world as he is loved by all!

visitor's guide

mascots

Bonhomme, our jolly snowman mascot is joined alongside a few animals that are local to Québec to lead the most popular activities and events at the Carnaval. Learn more about his friends through our map!

effigies

This is the Carnaval effigy, your physical souvenir and ticket into the Carnaval! Designed differently every year, it celebrates the year's festivies in the form of an ornament-like pendant with Bonhomme.

activities

From classic Canadian treats and dog sledding to outdoor EDM concerts and parades, the Carnaval is full of endless fun and new experiences for you and your friends and family to enjoy for the nine days!
